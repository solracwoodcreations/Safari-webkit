My Woodworking Journey

SMWC September 13, 2025

How healing after dying at the scene of a car accident and being revived on the way to the hospital influenced my woodworking journey.

It's true I could no longer do the construction work and pool cleaning jobs that I used to do, but that’s not why I started making wood projects. After my accident, I spent almost a year in the hospital and another half a year in a wheelchair. I had broken my pelvis, which was fixed with five screws holding it together, also crushed my T12 vertebrate to dust which was also fixed with a back fusion operation.

Between my pelvis operation and my back fusion, due to the fact that I had collapsed one of my lungs during the accident they cut me through the side of my body instead of cutting me through the back resulting in a total of 65 staples not stitches but big ugly giant staples. I looked like a human zipper. Anyways, that’s a story for another time. This is a story of a little wooden paper towel holder.
